Oil surges and stocks slide as the US strikes Iran again

A fresh round of US airstrikes reopened the market's biggest tail risk just as investors braced for the Fed.

Oil prices climbed after the United States launched new airstrikes on Iran in retaliation for attacks on commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz, with Brent rising roughly 2% toward $76 a barrel and the Treasury revoking a waiver that had let Tehran sell its oil. Equity futures slipped and semiconductors led losses, as investors rotated out of AI and technology names into healthcare and financials. The strikes landed hours before the release of the June Fed minutes, with markets now pricing better-than-even odds of a September rate hike.
