Lead story

Chip stocks slide as Samsung's profit surge fails to satisfy

Even a nineteenfold jump in quarterly profit wasn't enough to clear the sky-high bar investors have set for the memory boom.

Samsung Electronics, the world's biggest memory maker, tumbled as much as 11% in Seoul after its quarterly report failed to impress traders despite profit surging nineteenfold. The selloff rippled outward, with peers Micron and Sandisk sliding more than 4% in US premarket trading and S&P 500 futures slipping. The reaction underscored how much optimism is now priced into the AI-driven chip rally — and how little room remains for anything short of a blowout.
