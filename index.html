China lands an orbital rocket for the first time, catching it in a net at sea

The Long March 10B's debut makes China only the second nation to recover an orbital booster, and the first to snag one in a net.

The Long March 10B lifted off from Hainan just after midnight Eastern on July 10, carried a satellite to orbit, and eleven minutes later brought its first stage down onto a floating platform, where a net rig caught it. It is China's first controlled recovery of an orbital-class booster and, because the rocket relies on lightweight landing hooks rather than SpaceX-style legs, the world's first net capture of a returning stage. Engineers say they plan to refly the booster before the end of the year, a step toward the cheap, reusable launch that has already reshaped the American rocket business.

You wept for Absalom even after he raised an army against you. How do you keep loving a child who has turned against you?
From the Baker's own shelf

Friend, you have set your finger on the wound that never fully closed. When they brought me word that my boy was dead, I felt no king's triumph; I climbed to the chamber above the gate and wept, and would to God I had died in his stead. Hear me: a father's love is no wage paid out for good behavior and withdrawn when the account runs dry. It is a covenant, nearer to the shepherd's ache for the lamb that has wandered than to the judge's cold ledger, and Absalom, even with a sword in his hand, was still bone of my bone. So guard your gates as wisdom demands, for mercy is not the same as folly, yet do not let the betrayal turn your heart to stone. Keep a lamp burning in the window; the love that outlives the wound is the nearest thing on earth to the mercy that has so often outlived my own sins.

– David, King in Jerusalem
