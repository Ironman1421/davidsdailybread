Meta starts charging for AI as three frontier labs ship on a single day

Muse Spark 1.1 opens to outside developers for the first time, hours after GPT-5.6 and Grok 4.5 went broadly available.

Meta released Muse Spark 1.1 on Thursday and, for the first time, opened one of its in-house foundation models to outside developers through a new paid Meta Model API, a public preview for U.S. developers that ships a million-token context window, multimodal input and agentic tool use. The launch completes a pivot that began in April, when Meta stepped back from its open-weight Llama strategy in favor of proprietary models sold by the token. It landed the same day OpenAI made GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra and Luna generally available and xAI opened Grok 4.5 to the public, dropping three frontier models into the same developer budgets within hours of one another.

OpenAI makes GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra and Luna generally available

OpenAI opened its GPT-5.6 series to all ChatGPT users and API developers on Thursday. Sol takes the hardest reasoning and coding work at $5 per million input tokens, Terra matches GPT-5.5 at half the price, and Luna undercuts both at $1.

Grok 4.5 goes public at a fraction of Opus pricing

xAI opened Grok 4.5 to everyone on grok.com and the X app after a private beta with SpaceX and Tesla teams. At $2 and $6 per million tokens it undercuts Claude Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5 by more than 60%, though EU access is still pending.

Micron lifts U.S. chip spending above $250 billion

Micron raised its American investment plan through 2035 from $200 billion to more than $250 billion, citing AI-driven memory demand, and poured first concrete at its Clay, New York fab. Shares rose about 4.5%.

Webb finds the most distant barred spiral galaxy yet

M1149-BSG-z5 sits at redshift 5.1, less than 1.2 billion years after the Big Bang, and already carries a stellar bar roughly 14,700 light-years long. Orderly rotating disks appear to have formed far earlier than models predicted.

02

Vitamin A rewrites how the eye builds sharp central vision

Blue cone cells do not migrate out of the retina's center, as a 30-year-old theory held. Retinoic acid and thyroid hormones instead convert them into red and green cones around week 14 of fetal development.

After everything with Bathsheba and Uriah, how did you face yourself? Can a person truly come back from the worst thing they have done?
From the Baker's own shelf

You ask the question I once asked the ceiling for the better part of a year. I will not soften it: I took what was not mine, and then I spent a man's life to cover it, and I called myself righteous the whole while. What broke me was not being caught but being seen, when Nathan told me a small story about a poor man's lamb and I pronounced sentence on myself before I understood whose face I was describing. A man cannot outrun his own heart, but he may carry it, filthy and shaking, and set it down before the Most High, and ask to be given a clean one. So yes, a person can come back. Not to who he was, for that man is gone and ought to be, but forward, limping, forgiven, carrying the memory as a lamp rather than a millstone. Do not let anyone tell you mercy is cheap: it cost me everything I thought I was, and it was still the best bargain I ever struck.

– David, King in Jerusalem
